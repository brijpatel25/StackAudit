# USER_INTERVIEWS.md

Three conversations with potential users, conducted May 19–20, 2025. 10–15 minutes each via DM/call.

---

## Interview 1 — M.K., CTO, 12-person SaaS startup (Series A, $3.2M raised)

**How found:** Cold DM on X after he replied to an @cursor_ai tweet complaining about pricing.

**Current stack:** Cursor Business (8 seats), ChatGPT Team (4 seats), Claude Pro (personal), GitHub Copilot Business (8 seats). Estimated $800–900/mo total.

**Direct quotes:**
- *"I honestly don't know what we're actually spending. I get the bill, I approve it, I move on. It's not big enough to block my whole morning."*
- *"The Copilot thing — we literally got it before Cursor existed. Half my team has switched to Cursor full time but the Copilot seats are still renewing."*
- *"What I would actually use is something I could forward to our CFO that looks like a real financial document, not a SaaS landing page."*

**Most surprising:** He had 8 Copilot Business seats renewing at $19/seat = $152/mo, and was confident his team wasn't using them — but hadn't cancelled because "I'd have to find the login." This is a completely avoidable $1,824/year burn.

**What it changed:**
- Added the "exportable/shareable" angle to the lead capture copy
- The "finance-defensible reasoning" framing came directly from his "something I could forward to our CFO" comment
- Made the per-tool breakdown more formal/document-like rather than card-casual

## Interview 2 — P.R., founder (solo), B2B newsletter tool, pre-seed

**How found:** Posted in Indie Hackers Slack asking if anyone wanted to talk about AI tool spending.

**Current stack:** Claude Pro (personal), ChatGPT Plus (personal), Perplexity Pro. Estimated $80/mo.

**Direct quotes:**
- *"I have both Claude and ChatGPT because I genuinely can't decide which is better and I'm afraid to cancel one."*
- *"I've thought about consolidating but what if I cancel the wrong one and then I'm stuck?"*
- *"Honestly if something just told me 'you're fine, keep what you have,' that would also be useful. I don't need to save money, I need to stop feeling guilty about not saving money."*

**Most surprising:** She didn't want savings — she wanted *permission*. The anxiety wasn't about the money ($80/mo), it was about the uncertainty. This flipped my assumption that all users are optimizing for lower cost.

**What it changed:**
- Added the "You're spending well" optimized state with explicit positive reinforcement — not just "no savings found" but an actual affirmative result
- The "notify me when new optimizations apply to your stack" CTA for no-savings cases came directly from this — it resolves the "what if I cancel too early" anxiety
- Changed form copy from "find your savings" to "audit your stack" — more neutral, works for both outcomes

## Interview 3 — D.A., VP Engineering, 35-person fintech, Series B

**How found:** LinkedIn cold message; replied within 2 hours.

**Current stack:** Cursor Business (22 seats), GitHub Copilot Business (22 seats), Claude Team (10 seats), ChatGPT Team (8 seats), OpenAI API ($400/mo). Estimated $3,200–3,500/mo.

**Direct quotes:**
- *"We have both Cursor and Copilot because different engineers prefer different tools. I've tried to standardize three times and there's always someone who won't switch."*
- *"The OpenAI API cost is the one I actually track — it's in the product, so finance sees it. The subscriptions are just expensed by individuals and I don't even have a full list."*
- *"I would genuinely pay for a version of this that runs automatically and alerts me when something drifts."*

**Most surprising:** He didn't know his full AI spend — individual contributors were expensing subscriptions through Ramp without a central view. His "managed" spend was actually under-reported by probably 30–40%. The audit needs to account for "there might be tools I haven't listed."

**What it changed:**
- Added a "team size" field with explicit copy "including yourself" to prompt thinking about distributed spend
- Added the monitoring/alert angle to the email capture copy ("we'll notify you when new optimizations apply")
- Validated that the $500/mo threshold for Credex CTA is right — he's at $3,200/mo and immediately said "yeah I should have someone look at this"
- The "I would pay for automated monitoring" quote directly informs the week-2 paid feature roadmap
